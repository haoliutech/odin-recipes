Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a donburi, or Japanese rice bowl dish, in which chicken, egg, sliced scallion (or sometimes regular onions), and other ingredients are all simmered together in a sauce and then served on top of a large bowl of rice. The name of the dish comes from the Japanese word for parent and child, because it uses both chicken and egg.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup dashi stock
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups cooked Japanese rice
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions

Steps

  1. Combine dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar in a small saucepan and bring to a boil over high heat. Add onion and reduce heat to low. Simmer until onion is tender, about 5 minutes. Add chicken and simmer until cooked through, about 5 minutes more.
  2. Pour eggs over chicken and onion. Cover and cook until eggs are set, about 3 minutes.
  3. Divide rice between 2 bowls. Top with chicken, onion, and egg mixture. Garnish with green onions.
